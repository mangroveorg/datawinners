Human Network International builds the capacity of development partners to collect data and make better decisions.

Featured clients:

The World Bank and the Government of Madagascar

The Government of Madagascar hired HNI for two separate projects using World Bank loans. The first was to create an application for the Ministry of Health to collect stock information for life-saving HIV drugs using SMS from a network of doctors authorized to administer the medications. The second was to work with the National Nutrition Office to collect monthly activity report data using SMS from a large network of community-based nutrition agents.
www.worldbank.org

Marie Stopes Madagascar

MSI is one of the largest international family planning organizations in the world.
MSI delivers quality family planning and reproductive healthcare to millions of the world's poorest and most vulnerable women. MSI/Madagascar hired HNI for three separate initiatives. The first application collects monthly client data using SMS from a network of private sector doctors grouped together under the brand “Blue Star”. The second collects client satisfaction data using SMS from women who visited clinics affiliated with Marie Stopes International. The third project allows clinics operated by Marie Stopes to submit monthly reports via the Internet.
www.mariestopes.org

RTI International

RTI conducts research and provides technical services to government agencies, universities, foundations, private businesses, and other organizations. HNI and RTI worked together on two different initiatives. The first application allows community-based supervisors to report monthly activity data using SMS. The second application allows RTI employees to submit their supervisory reports and training results using SMS.
www.rti.org

Conservation International

CI is a nonprofit organization, which applies innovations in science, economics, policy and community participation to protect the Earth's biodiversity around the world. CI hired HNI to help build their internal capacity to collect small grants information using SMS. HNI also created a second application that allows National Parks Staff to use SMS to report on illegal activities in any of Madagascar’s protected areas.
www.conservation.org

National Bureau of Risk and Disaster Management

Catholic Relief Services (CRS) hired HNI to build the internal capacity of Madagascar’s National Bureau of Risk and Disaster Management (known by the French acronym BNGRC) to collect critical damage reports from post-disaster sites using SMS.
www.bngrc.mg

Ambatovy

Ambatovy is a large-tonnage, long-life nickel and cobalt mining enterprise located in Madagascar. It is the largest-ever foreign investment in the country and will soon rank among the largest nickel mining entities in the world. Ambatovy hired HNI to help collect grievance reports from the field.
www.ambatovy.com
